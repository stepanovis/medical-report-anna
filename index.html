Key Problems — Detailed Analysis

1. Chronic Endometritis

The endometrium is the "soil" where the embryo implants. Chronic inflammation disrupts receptivity. Studies show it reduces implantation rates 2–3x and is a recognized cause of recurrent pregnancy loss.

Diagnosis: Visual signs on hysteroscopy (Dr. Diler, 21 Jan 2026). Standard histology showed "proliferative endometrium without atypias" but standard H&E staining often misses chronic endometritis. The gold standard is CD138 immunohistochemistry (plasma cell marker).

Treatment protocol:

  • Doxycycline 100 mg × 2/day × 14 days (some protocols add metronidazole)
  • Post-treatment: repeat biopsy with CD138 or repeat hysteroscopy to confirm resolution
  • Do NOT attempt conception until confirmed cured
  • Total timeline: ~2 months (treatment + recovery + verification)

2. Hyperhomocysteinemia + B12 Deficiency

Homocysteine is metabolized using vitamins B12, folate, and B6. When deficient (or with MTHFR gene polymorphism), homocysteine accumulates.

Risks during conception and pregnancy:

  • Impaired embryo implantation
  • 2–3x increased early miscarriage risk (homocysteine >15)
  • Neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly) — most dangerous
  • Preeclampsia, placental abruption, fetal growth restriction
  • Impaired oocyte quality

B12 = 236 pg/mL with elevated homocysteine = confirmed functional deficiency despite being "in range."

Action:

  • Methylcobalamin (active B12) 1000–5000 μg/day
  • L-methylfolate (5-MTHF) 800–1000 μg/day (NOT regular folic acid — may not be metabolized if MTHFR mutation present)
  • Pyridoxine (B6) 25–50 mg/day
  • MTHFR genetic test (C677T and A1298C)
  • Recheck homocysteine in 6–8 weeks. Target: <10 μmol/L
